## Key Takeaways

- **Equipment capabilities**: Three smaller Konica color printers, Production 300 B&W printer (up to 12x18), roll-fed printer with inline perforation, and multiple inserters ranging from 2,000-12,000 envelopes/hour
- **Presort operation**: Designated Detached Mail Unit (DMU) processing ~50,000 letters/hour with address verification and move-update checks; nine drivers covering Manhattan, KS to Blue Springs and south to Riley County area
- **Pricing model**: Charge ~2¢ per piece for presort, capturing the spread between standard (68.6¢) and 5-digit sorted rates (59¢), yielding ~8¢ margin on qualified mail
- **Major clients**: USDA (5,000-10,000 letters/day), SunLife, State of Kansas, HCA, Advent Health, Swope Health, Children's Mercy Physicians, DFA, Tallgrass
- **Competitive landscape**: Pitney Bowes Presort is primary competitor (ships mail to Omaha for sorting); recently acquired several Strong customers who prefer local processing
- **Turnaround commitment**: Mail received by 5 PM ships same day; local mail bypasses distribution center for faster delivery
- **Quality control**: Multi-level sign-off process (1 or 2 signatures) based on job criticality; serial number tracking throughout production; ERP system status visibility for customer service
