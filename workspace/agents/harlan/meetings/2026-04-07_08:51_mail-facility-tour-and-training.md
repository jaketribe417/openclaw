# Mail facility tour and training

**Date:** Tuesday, April 7, 2026 at 08:51 AM
**Duration:** Unknown
**Meeting ID:** `7399692`

## Summary

## Summary

Jason gave a comprehensive facility tour to Ben, a new sales trainee from the Little Rock office, walking through the company's print and mail production operations. The tour covered the full workflow from data processing through printing, inserting, and pre-sort operations. Jason explained the technical capabilities of various machines including Mach 6/7 inkjet printers (3,000-10,000 envelopes/hour), roll-to-roll inkjet systems (20,000 sheets/hour), and multiple inserter lines handling 2,000-12,000 pieces per hour. He detailed how barcode tracking ensures quality control and error correction throughout production, and explained postal discount strategies—how pre-sorting can reduce postage from 74¢ to 59¢ for letters and from $1.67 to 97¢ for flats. Jason also discussed the three customer service tiers: full outsourcing, meter-only service, and pre-sort-only service for customers who produce mail in-house. Ben shadowed veteran operator Guthrie to observe real production workflow after the tour.

## Atmosphere

Professional yet informal training environment—conversational, patient, and educational. Jason clearly enjoys explaining the operations and takes pride in the facility's capabilities. Ben is engaged, asks thoughtful questions, and acknowledges he's in learning mode. The production floor has an active, industrious feel with operators like Todd, Anthony, and Kelte working their stations.

## Key Take aways

- **Mach 7 upgrade coming**: New printer will boost envelope speed from 3,000 to 10,000/hour
- **Tyson Foods deal closing**: Potential Mach 7 sale to Tyson Foods in northwest Arkansas via former Canon contact and local AM Jodi—expected to sign in April
- **Sunlight customer visit tomorrow**: Joint customer with G5s and Lineage Accelerate Office equipment; also a pre-sort pickup customer
- **Major competitive advantage**: One of few Kansas City providers offering same-day flat sorting (9x12 documents) with significant postage savings
- **File Base automation launching later 2025**: Will enable automatic error reporting from machines back to data processing
- **Peak capacity context**: Facility hit 1 million pieces in one week before Christmas; normal volume 400-500K/week; substantial unused capacity
- **Cross-training important**: New print operator starting next week to add redundancy; operators like Kelte and Todd primarily run specific machines but can cover elsewhere
- **SOC 2 Type 2 audited**: Security and process controls formally verified annually
- **Arkansas market opportunity**: Arkansas Mailing in Little Rock identified as competitor doing similar pre-sort work—potential prospect for Lineage
