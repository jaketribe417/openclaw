## Summary
Jason conducted a comprehensive training session on the company's "Midnight" ERP system for a new team member (likely Ryan, with Brian also present). The session covered the system's architecture, which integrates Salesforce for sales funnel management, Midnight for operations/ERP, and QuickBooks for financials. Jason walked through key modules including real-time employee activity tracking, job costing, estimates and orders, inventory management, and postage escrow systems. He emphasized how estimates convert to live orders, the template system for recurring monthly jobs, and the importance of accurate postage tracking given the high financial stakes.

## Atmosphere
Collaborative and educational with an engaged trainee actively asking clarifying questions. The tone was patient and thorough, with moments of enthusiasm when demonstrating system capabilities ("That's super cool," "Pretty cool, yeah"). Jason balanced high-level overviews with practical operational details, frequently checking for understanding.

## Key Takeaways
- Midnight ERP integrates three systems: Salesforce (sales), Midnight (operations), QuickBooks (financials)
- Real-time dashboard shows employee activity and job assignments; zeros indicate active work not yet completed
- Estimates are pre-jobs; only become live orders when exported, triggering billing and production tracking
- Print services are the most complex pricing component with calculator tools for different press configurations
- S1 services = non-billable tracking services; X1 services = operator instructions appearing on work orders
- Template orders auto-generate ~500 recurring monthly jobs, eliminating manual CSR work
- Orders contain "versions" for daily jobs under monthly parent orders (e.g., Lucid's structure)
- Postage escrow system manages large customer prepayments; postage accuracy is critical due to high financial risk and thin margins
- New postage system implementation is underway to replace 30-year-old "Postage Recon" system
- Inventory is currently tracked at warehouse level by Dave, not yet at individual job level—area identified for improvement

## Action Items
- Follow-up deep-dive session needed with Ryan on print pricing/markups (estimated 2-hour discussion)
- Continue work on implementing job-level inventory tracking
- Complete transition to new postage system with contracted vendor
