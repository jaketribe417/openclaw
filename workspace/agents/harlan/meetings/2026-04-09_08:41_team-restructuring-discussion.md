## Summary

## Summary

Jason participated in a lengthy business meeting covering organizational restructuring, staffing decisions, vendor negotiations, and strategic planning for Lineage Connect. The meeting addressed posting a new analyst position, transitioning roles for Heather and Kim, establishing a consulting arrangement with departing employee Bob for envelope sales expertise, and implementing EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System) principles. Jason also observed a vendor meeting with George from Midland Paper discussing paper pricing, allocation risks, and supply chain forecasting amid industry-wide price increases and potential shortages.

## Atmosphere

Professional and collaborative with moments of tension around staffing changes and vendor transitions. The tone shifted to educational and relationship-focused during the paper vendor discussion, with an underlying urgency about supply chain planning and cost management.

## Key Takeaways

- **Organizational Restructuring**: Jason presented a new org chart for the CSR team with Jamie as lead, three CSRs in KC, Shaylin leading Topeka, a new analyst handling postage/reconciliation, and a front desk support role; discussed transitioning Heather to report to Sabrina and phasing out Kim's role
- **Bob's Consulting Agreement**: Negotiated terms for Bob to stay on as consultant for envelope expertise—small monthly retainer plus 3% finder's fee for letter shop referrals and 5% for envelope sales, with additional hourly rate ($50/hour suggested) for ongoing contract support work
- **Staffing Decisions**: Approved posting the analyst position quickly; agreed to have Joe handle difficult conversations with Heather and Kim in person in two weeks; discussed replacing Jeff with someone more capable for material handling/receiving
- **Paper Vendor Meeting**: George from Midland Paper provided extensive market intelligence—Domtar and other mills implementing price increases, allocation risks for Sappi and others, importance of forecasting and building purchase history to secure allocation rights
- **Equipment & Operations**: Discussed Mach 7 capabilities for small-run envelopes, evaluating new printer options (Canon, Ricoh, HP), and frustration with Blue Crest's high pricing for equipment moves ($26K each for breakdown and installation)
- **Compliance Urgency**: HR flagged that many employees hadn't completed required training due tomorrow; Jason already completed his; group discussed forcing scheduled completion times rather than chasing people down
- **EOS Implementation**: Brian (new operations leader) attended EOS session and is reading Traction; team getting him a binder; John (EOS consultant) not attending next meeting

## Action Items

- Post analyst position immediately after conversation with Heather
- Update Bob's consulting agreement to include hourly rate clause and send for review
- Schedule face-to-face conversations with Kim and Heather in two weeks to explain new organizational structure
- Push Blue Crest equipment decision to late May to evaluate alternatives and pricing
- Implement scheduled training completion times for compliance requirements
- Review SharePoint files for envelope contract information with Bob
- Forecast paper needs and secure POs ahead of anticipated price increases and allocation constraints
