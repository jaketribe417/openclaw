## Key Takeaways

- **Midnight System**: Jason demonstrated the full estimating workflow including the print calculator (selecting printer templates, paper sizes, color specs), understanding cost components (press cost + click cost), and how markup percentages translate to customer pricing. Key detail: 36,000 sheets needed for 18,000 two-page letters when calculating folding/inserting quantities.

- **Salesforce Structure**: Opportunities are named Year-Customer-Project (e.g., "2026 Missionary Letters") for easy scanning. Annual revenue values are used rather than total contract value. Forecasting requires both rep and manager commitment. "Big hits" are deals >$50K. P1/P2/P3 pipeline stages auto-update based on close dates.

- **Pricing Strategy**: Standard retail pricing exists but flexibility is needed for competitive bids. Jason noted they spent 18 months analyzing market positioning for government and commercial bids. Paper costs ~5% annual increase, services ~2.5-3%.

- **Lead Sources**: Lineage Connect (hardware sales partners) brings opportunities for 3% commission—typically 4-5 print/mail deals annually per partner. Web leads, cold calls, and trade shows are also tracked.

- **Chamber Memberships**: Topeka Chamber (~$500/year, ~1 lead/month) and other KC metro chambers under evaluation. Hannah suggested Financial Executives International Chamber (CFO-focused) as high-value based on prior experience.

- **Production Context**: Brief insight into operational challenges—an 8,000-piece job had equipment compatibility issues requiring potential reprint and same-day delivery tradeoffs.
