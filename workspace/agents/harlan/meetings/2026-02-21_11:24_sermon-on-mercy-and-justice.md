## Key Takeaways

- The Hebrew text of Jonah 4:1 reveals Jonah's call for judgment against Nineveh's "wickedness" (ra'ah) actually ignited that same wickedness within himself
- Ancient Israel's complaint was not that God was too violent, but that he wasn't violent enough toward their enemies
- Modern manifestations of "the spirit of Jonah" include justifying others' suffering based on their choices, rhetoric, or legal status
- Every injustice begins with dehumanizing others by finding reasons to withhold mercy
- The gospel demands consistency: receiving mercy for oneself while denying it to others is spiritually incoherent
- God's provision of the plant was to save Jonah from his own wickedness, not merely physical discomfort
- The 120,000 "people who do not know their right hand from their left" in Nineveh likely refers to children, emphasizing God's compassion for the innocent alongside the guilty
