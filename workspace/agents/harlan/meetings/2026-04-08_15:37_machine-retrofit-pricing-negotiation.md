# Machine retrofit pricing negotiation

**Date:** Wednesday, April 8, 2026 at 03:37 PM
**Duration:** Unknown
**Meeting ID:** `7427666`

## Summary

## Summary

Jason participated in a tense business negotiation call with Blue Crest (mail processing equipment vendor) regarding the purchase and retrofit of a used Rival inserter system. The conversation centered on unexpectedly high costs to make the machine operational for Lineage Connect's needs. Jason pushed back aggressively on $109K in programming and hardware costs, $46K for file-based processing infrastructure, and $28K for deinstallation/reinstallation—arguing these fees were excessive and threatened to walk away from the deal or find alternative technicians. The call ended with the vendor agreeing to consolidate quotes and revisit pricing, followed by a brief post-meeting discussion with a colleague about Kansas City restaurants and weather.

## Atmosphere

Professional but increasingly confrontational and frustrated, particularly during pricing negotiations. Jason was direct and assertive, using strong language ("highway robbery," "ridiculous fees") while maintaining a business-focused tone. The vendor representatives became defensive but ultimately cooperative. Post-meeting tone shifted to casual and friendly.

## Key Takeaways

- **Equipment Purchase**: Lineage Connect bought a used Blue Crest Rival inserter from a US Treasury auction to replace Bell & Howell equipment and standardize on Blue Crest technology
- **Cost Dispute**: Vendor quoted $109K to retrofit (new hard drives, programming, professional services), $46K for file-based processing enablement, and $28K for move/reassembly—totaling ~$183K before additional options
- **Jason's Position**: Argued the pricing defeats the purpose of buying used equipment; stated he could sell the machine at profit and stay with Bell & Howell or find independent technicians to do the work cheaper
- **Technical Requirements**: Need file-based processing on both Rival and existing Evolution systems; current Rival has outdated output stacker incompatible with camera-based verification
- **Strategic Context**: Limited budget prioritizing printer purchase; this was meant as a cost-effective entry point to Blue Crest ecosystem with plans to expand later
- **Negotiation Outcome**: Vendor (Dennis) agreed to go to leadership to reduce pricing and consolidate quotes; Jason requested combined quote for file-based + retrofit work, cycle count verification, and exploration of lease financing
- **Side Issues**: Hunkler unwinder/cutter may be usable for roll-fed printing; meter on machine is end-of-life; printer quote still pending test job results

## Action Items

- Vendor to provide consolidated quote combining file-based infrastructure ($46K) and retrofit work ($109K) with reduced pricing
- Obtain machine cycle count from Philadelphia service tech (Mike Coral) to verify low usage
- Clarify $28K deinstallation quote scope and timeline
- Explore lease financing options through vendor's technology finance group
- Get quote for converting pin-feed cutter to pinless for roll-fed operation
- Follow up on separate printer quote pending test job completion after vendor's innovation summit
- Consider approaching Hunkler directly for cutter conversion work
