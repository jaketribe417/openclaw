## Summary

Duane called to vouch for his close friend Bill, who runs a flat processing operation co-located in Duane's Marion/Wayne buildings. Duane explained their 20-year business relationship: they previously turned down a joint acquisition offer because neither would sell without the other's agreement. Bill's business is now struggling due to industry changes—bundle size regulations shifting from 10 to 50 pieces (which Jason confirmed significantly hurt his own operations too), plus a major customer moving 80-90% to email. Bill is exploring whether Jason's company could process his flats, though this would likely mean laying off 4 of his 5 employees, which he has been avoiding despite losing money. Duane asked Jason and Richard to contact Bill directly, with Bill promising to loop Duane back in before any final decision.

## Atmosphere

Cautiously warm and trust-building. Duane was deliberately establishing personal credibility after a previous negative experience with Jason's company, framing this as a step-by-step relationship journey. The tone was confidential and empathetic, particularly around the difficult human impact of potential layoffs.

## Key Takeaways

- **New business opportunity**: Potential partnership or acquisition of Bill's flat processing operation
- **Industry pressure**: Bundle size changes (10→50) and email migration are squeezing traditional mail processors
- **Volume reality**: Jason confirmed his own pre-sort pickup volume is declining year-over-year; growth only coming from internal print/mail work
- **Duane's positioning**: Has existing relationship with Iowa Mail as competitor/alternative; sends them out-of-state pre-sort only, no flats
- **Decision structure**: Bill will not finalize anything without consulting Duane first

- **Follow up with Duane** after Bill conversation (Bill will update Duane regardless, but Jason's team can also loop back)
